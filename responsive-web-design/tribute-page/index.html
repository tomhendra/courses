A Timeline of Charles Darwin's Life

  • 1809 - Born on 12 February in The Mount, Shrewsbury, England.
  • 1825 - Spent the summer as an apprentice doctor, before attending the University of Edinburgh Medical School.
  • 1828 - Father sent Darwin to Christ's College, Cambridge, to study for a BA degree to become an Anglican country parson, as a consequence of neglecting medical studies.
  • 1831 - Graduated from Cambridge to join a survey voyage on HMS Beagle, circumnavigating the globe for 5 years.
  • 1836 - Returned to England as a celebrity in scientific circles.
  • 1839 - Married Emma Wedgwood & published Journal of Researches.
  • 1842 - Coral Reefs published; Darwin moved to Down House in Kent.
  • 1846 - Undertook an exhaustive taxonomical study of barnacles (Cirripedes)
  • 1859 - On the Origin of Species published.
  • 1870 - The Beagle is sold, probably for scrap.
  • 1871 - The Descent of Man published.
  • 1872 - The Expression of the Emotions in Man and Animals published.
  • 1876 - Autobiography published.
  • 1877 - The University of Cambridge awarded Charles Darwin an honorary doctorate.
  • 1880 - The Power of Movement in Plants published.
  • 1881 - Final work, The Formation of Vegetable Mould through the Action of Worms publsihed.
  • 1882 - Darwin died at his home, Down House, Kent aged 73. The funeral was attended by thousands of people, including scientists, philosophers and dignitaries.
